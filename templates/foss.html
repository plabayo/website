ラマ

Our main open source focus is Rama (ラマ), a Rust project that we spend most of our time on. Rama is a powerful modular service framework designed specifically for the Rust language. Its primary focus is to develop proxies, but its versatile nature allows it to excel in developing web services and clients as well. In fact, this website is served by Rama.

With Rama, you can move and transform your network packets with ease. Its modular design allows for flexible customization, making it an ideal solution for a wide range of network-related tasks. Whether you need to develop proxies, web services, or clients, Rama is an excellent choice.

Contact us today to learn more about how Rama can help you with your network-related needs.

To learn more about why we developed Rama, you can listen to the Rama episode on Rustacean Station . This episode provides valuable insights into the development process and the benefits of using Rama to develop proxy services.

Rust Crates

Besides Rama we have a couple of more Rust crates that we develop and maintain:

  • tokio-graceful : Graceful shutdown util for Rust projects using the Tokio Async runtime.
  • venndb : in memory Rust database to query your data like a Venn diagram .

At our team, we're passionate about Rust and its ecosystem. That's why we're proud to contribute to all kinds of Rust crates, pushing fixes upstream, proposing and developing features, reviewing PRs, and helping community members online.

By working together with and for the Rust ecosystem, we're able to help ensure that Rust remains a reliable and secure language for years to come. Our team has the expertise and experience needed to provide a wide range of Rust-related services, including team training, code auditing, codebase refactoring, and project development.
